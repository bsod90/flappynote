Help & Instructions

Learn how to use Vocal Trainer's pitch training tools

Requirements

  • Microphone access - Both tools need to hear your voice
  • Modern browser - Chrome, Safari, Firefox, or Edge (latest versions)
  • Quiet environment - Background noise can affect pitch detection
  • Headphones recommended - To avoid feedback from the drone

Note: If opening from social media apps (Facebook, Instagram, etc.), tap the menu and select "Open in Browser" for microphone access to work properly.

Flappy Note

A singing game where you control a bird by matching pitches. Guide the bird through gates by singing the target notes displayed on each gate.

How to Play

  1. Click Start Game and allow microphone access when prompted
  2. The bird follows your voice - sing higher to go up, lower to go down
  3. Each gate shows a target note (Do, Re, Mi, etc.) - sing that note to pass through
  4. Match the pitch shown on the gate to score points
  5. The game ends if you miss a gate or go off-screen
Tip: Start with the Major Scale on a comfortable root note (like D or E). Enable the Root Drone to help you hear the tonal center.

Settings

Setting Description
Root Note The tonal center of the scale. Choose based on your vocal range.
Mode The scale or chord pattern to practice. Start with Major Scale.
Direction Ascending (up), Descending (down), or Random order of scale degrees.
Root Drone Plays a sustained root note for pitch reference.

Scoring

  • Pass through a gate while matching the pitch = +1 point
  • The closer you are to the target pitch, the better
  • Try to complete the full scale without missing any gates

Vocal Monitor

A real-time pitch visualization tool that displays your voice on a piano roll. Watch your pitch as a continuous line over time.

How to Use

  1. Click Start to begin recording
  2. Sing or hum - your pitch appears as an orange line on the piano roll
  3. The line thickness indicates detection confidence
  4. Scale degree rows are highlighted with colors matching the keyboard
  5. Click Clear to reset the display

Piano Roll Features

  • Left keyboard - Shows note names and solfege syllables (Do, Re, Mi...)
  • Colored rows - Scale degrees are highlighted; gray hatched rows are out-of-scale notes
  • Click & drag on keys - Play reference notes (glide across keys for portamento effect)
  • Scroll/drag the main area - Navigate through your recording history
  • Mouse wheel - Scroll through time
Tip: Use this tool to visualize vibrato, pitch slides, and intonation. The piano roll shows exactly how your voice moves between notes.

Settings

Setting Description
Root Note Sets which note is "Do" and determines scale highlighting.
Scale Determines which notes are highlighted on the piano roll.
Root Drone Plays a sustained root note for pitch reference.

General Tips

Getting Better Pitch Detection

  • Sing clearly with good breath support
  • Avoid very quiet or very loud singing
  • Use headphones to prevent the drone from interfering with detection
  • Find a quiet room with minimal background noise
  • Hold your device at a consistent distance from your mouth

Practice Suggestions

  • Start simple - Begin with Major Scale ascending before trying modes or chords
  • Use the drone - It helps establish the key center in your ear
  • Slow down - Focus on accuracy over speed
  • Record yourself - Use Vocal Monitor to spot patterns in your intonation
  • Practice intervals - Try 7th chords to work on larger jumps

Keyboard Shortcuts

Shortcut Action
Ctrl/Cmd + Shift + D Toggle debug overlay (shows pitch detection details)
